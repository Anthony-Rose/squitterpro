Not another live map

tar1090 and Virtual Radar Server already show you what's overhead right now. SquitterPro is about what your station has accomplished.

📒

A life list for aircraft

Every airframe you've ever received, with photo, operator, type, and how many times it's visited. Like an eBird life list, for your antenna.

🚁

Military traffic, obvious at a glance

Fighter and attack-helicopter icons in olive drab, driven by ICAO type data — Black Hawks look like Black Hawks, not like Cessnas. Filter to just the traffic you care about.

📡

Range records that mean something

All-time furthest reception with the aircraft's full details at the record moment, plus a 360° coverage envelope built from your receiver's own statistics. Decode-glitch filtering keeps the bragging rights honest.

🔔

Alerts while you live your life

SquitterPro sits in the system tray. Windows notifications when a watched aircraft returns, a drone shows up, someone squawks 7700, or you set a new distance record.

📈

Trends over time

Distinct aircraft per hour, day, or year by category. Are military flights increasing? More drones this month? The chart answers.

🗻

2.5-D altitude view

An isometric view of your airspace with every aircraft on a stem at its true altitude — see the vertical structure of your local traffic, plus area METAR weather.

Works with what you have — even nothing

Native support for dedicated receivers, Raspberry Pi trackers, and free community feeds if you haven't bought hardware yet.

Built by a receiver owner, for receiver owners

SquitterPro is built in Jamul, California by Anthony Rose — a retired Navy EOD technician who put an ADS-B receiver on the roof and wanted to know what was flying over the house. San Diego airspace obliged: airliners into KSAN, Black Hawks and Super Hornets out of Miramar and North Island, and the occasional surprise from far out over the Pacific.

Nothing existing kept score — the live maps forgot every aircraft the moment it left the screen. So SquitterPro treats your antenna like a birder treats binoculars: every contact goes in the book.

SquitterPro is an independent one-person project, built and supported directly by its maker.
